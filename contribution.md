## Contributing Guidelines

### Adding New Challenges or Ideas

Place new challenges in the appropriate existing category:

- **Programs**: Algorithms, logic, and small code challenges (not full apps or games)  
- **Applications**: Full applications with features and persistence  
- **Games**: Simple or more complex game implementations  
- **Miscellaneous**: Anything that doesn’t fit the above  

If a new category seems needed, suggest it—but keep categories broad and avoid overly specific ones.

### Writing Guides or Explanations

When adding a guide or explanation:

- Place it inside the challenge's folder.  
- Name it `Guide.md`.  
- Include sources or references (no URL shorteners; use full, trusted URLs).  
- Explain your reasoning, assumptions, and steps, not just instructions. Use clear inference and visualization whenever possible.  

> A good guide doesn't just show *how* but explains *why* and *when*. Most people read tutorials because they don't understand the concept yet—help them learn by breaking down ideas clearly.  

- Write for beginners or people new to the topic.

### What Not to Do

- **Do not submit fully completed solutions** as pull requests. This repo is mainly a list of challenges, no solutions. It’s public to inspire others and be used as a template.  
- Avoid overcomplicating explanations. Clarity and simplicity are key. Detailed reasoning is encouraged, but keep explanations straightforward and easy to follow.
